Power Suply Unit and control box

You will fin here how to make the box for PSU and control.

Step 1: Cut the PMMA sheets

Cut the 5 mm PMMA sheets (2 sheets should be enough). Files are available for laser cutter with DXF format by clicking here, and the FreeCAD files are availables by clicking here if you want to make any change or export the files in another format.

These pieces were cut on a 60W CO2 laser cutter, with the following parameters (note that the parameters depend on the machine and its laser power):
Speed : 5 mm/s;
 Power : 100%.

Step 2: Glue the PMMA sheets

For this step, we recommend to assemble the box in a first place to make sure the cut work properly, and to avoid gluing parts the wrong way. On the following image, the pieces to glue are shown with red edges where glue should be added.

Glue the PMMA sheets you just cut with glue for acrylic sheets. For clean gluing, use masking tape on borders near the parts were the glue is applied.

Step 3: Add the feet

First, 3D print 4 3D printed feet. Insert 4x M3 nuts into the feet, then screw the feet with x4 M3x8mm screws onto the PMMA box.

Step 4: Power supply units

Attach the PSU for RaspberryPi with 2x M3x8mm screws. Then attach the PSU for CNC shield and motors with M4x10mm screws.

Step 5: Power outlet

Simply attache the power outlet with 2x M3x8mm screws and 2x M3 nuts.

Step 6: Wiring

To wire the PSUs to the power outlet, you will need a minimum of 45 cm of blue cable, and brown cable and yellow cable. For each one, you can cut: * 30 cm to connect the power outler to the PSU for CNC shield and motors; * 15 cm to connect both PSU. Strip the cables on about 10 mm (for example with crimping pliers). On 15 cm long cables, crimp the fitting ferrules on each end of the cables. On 30 cm long cables, crimp ferrules on 1 end, and crimp connectors on the other end of the cables.

Step 7: Fan

Attach the fan with 4x M3x18mm screws and M3 nuts.

Step 8: Separation between PSU and CTRL

Step 9: Control : RaspberryPi

Attach the RaspberryPi with 4x M2x20 mm screws and 4x M2 nuts, adding 4x 3D printed M2x10mm spacers. Finally, connect the RPi to the PSU for RaspberryPi with these wires.

Step 10: Control : Arduino

Beside the RaspberryPi, attach the Arduino board with 4x M2x20 mm screws and 4x M2 nuts, adding 4x 3D printed M2x10mm spacers.

Step 11: CNC shield

Cut 35 cm of cables, strip it on 10 mm with the crimping pliers, then add the ferrules.
Plug the CNC shield onto the Arduino board. Make sure to plug the A4899 drivers correctly on the CNC shield.
Make sure you connect the black wire to -Vo on the PSU for CNC shield and motors, and red wire to +Vo. The similarly connect the red wire to + on the CNC shield, and black wire to -.

Step 12: PSU lid

Attach the emergency button to the PSU lid. Connect these wires to the emergency button, then to the CNC shield, on "E-STOP" pins.
Then fix 4x 3D printed attaches with M3x10mm screws and M3 nuts.

Step 13: Control lid

Attach 4x 3D printed attaches on the control lid, with M3x10mm screws and M3 nuts. Then attach the touch screen with 4x M3 nuts, 4x M3x18mm screws and 4x 3D printed M3 spacers.

Step 14: Close the box

If you want to close the box, then you will need 8x M3x10mm screws and M3 nuts more.

Connect everything

  • Connect the RaspberryPi to the Arduino board with a USB cable;
  • Connect the RaspberryPi the the screen with an HDMI cable (micro-HDMI connector for the RPi4);
  • Connect the RaspberryPi to the screen with a micro-USB cable (for touch screen)
  • OPTIONAL : connect a mouse to the RaspberryPi;
  • OPTIONAL : connect a keyboard to the RaspberryPi;
  • OPTIONAL : connect a bigger monitor to the RaspberryPi.
